# Lesson 10 — Writing structure

**Level:** Elementary–Pre-intermediate  
**Time:** 25–30 minutes  
**Skills:** Writing, Grammar, Vocabulary

---

## What you will learn

- How to structure a short paragraph
- How to use correct punctuation
- How to write clearly about yourself and familiar topics
- A step-by-step method for writing in a test

---

## Part 1 — What the writing test looks like

You will usually be asked to write **6–10 sentences** about a familiar topic. Common prompts include:

- *Write about yourself and your family.*
- *Describe your typical day.*
- *Write about where you come from.*
- *Explain why you want to learn English.*

> 💡 **You are not expected to write an essay.** A short, clear, and accurate paragraph is better than a long, confusing one.

---

## Part 2 — A simple 3-part structure

Every good paragraph has three parts:

| Part | Purpose | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Opening sentence** | Introduce the topic | *My name is Rani and I am from India.* |
| **Middle sentences (3–6)** | Give details and examples | *I have been in NZ for two years. I live with my husband and son...* |
| **Closing sentence** | End with a thought, wish, or summary | *I am grateful to be in New Zealand and I hope to improve my English this year.* |

---

## Part 3 — Model paragraphs

### Prompt: "Write about yourself and your family."

**Beginner version:**

> My name is Jong-Hee. I am from South Korea. I am 34 years old. I have a husband and one son. My son is 6 years old. I want to learn English.

**Elementary version:**

> My name is Jong-Hee Kim. I am from Seoul, in South Korea. I came to Auckland two years ago with my husband and our son, who is now 6 years old. My husband works in construction and I stay at home to look after our son. I am studying English at DynaSpeak so that I can communicate better and help my son when he starts school. I enjoy cooking Korean food and going for walks in the park near our home.

**Pre-intermediate version:**

> My name is Jong-Hee Kim and I originally come from Seoul, the capital city of South Korea. I moved to Auckland two years ago with my husband Junsu and our six-year-old son. Life in New Zealand is very different from Korea — the pace is slower and the environment is much more relaxed. My husband works in construction, and I am currently at home looking after our son. I decided to join DynaSpeak because I want to become more confident in English so I can communicate with teachers, doctors, and other parents in my community. In my spare time, I enjoy cooking, visiting local markets, and exploring different parts of Auckland. My goal this year is to complete Level 3 English and perhaps find a part-time job.

## Part 4 — Punctuation rules

These are the most important punctuation rules for your writing test.

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| Start every sentence with a **capital letter** | **I** am from Nepal. |
| End every sentence with a **full stop (.)** | I live in Auckland**.** |
| Use a **capital letter** for names and places | My name is **Maria**. I live in **Māngere**. |
| Use a **capital letter** for the word "I" | **I** am a student. |
| Use a **comma (,)** in lists | I enjoy cooking**,** reading**,** and walking. |
| Use a **comma** before linking words like "but" | I miss my country**,** but I am happy here. |

---

## Part 5 — Common writing mistakes

### Mistake 1: No capital letter at the start

❌ *my name is ali.*  
✅ ***M**y name is **A**li.*

### Mistake 2: No full stop at the end

❌ *I live in Auckland I have two children*  
✅ *I live in Auckland**.**  I have two children**.***

### Mistake 3: Wrong verb form

❌ *I am live in Glen Eden.*  
✅ *I **live** in Glen Eden.*

### Mistake 4: Writing in your language word order

❌ *I yesterday went to the shops.*  
✅ *I went to the shops **yesterday**.*

### Mistake 5: One very long sentence

❌ *I am from the Philippines and I moved to Auckland and I have two children and I am studying English and I want to find a job.*  
✅ Break it into shorter sentences:  
*I am from the Philippines. I moved to Auckland three years ago. I have two children. I am studying English at DynaSpeak so that I can find a better job.*

---

## Part 6 — The writing checklist

Before you finish, check:

- [ ] Does every sentence start with a capital letter?
- [ ] Does every sentence end with a full stop?
- [ ] Are names and places capitalised?
- [ ] Have you used the correct verb tense?
- [ ] Does every sentence have a subject and a verb?
- [ ] Have you answered the question that was asked?
- [ ] Are your sentences clear and easy to understand?
- [ ] Have you used some linking words (because, and, but, also)?

---

## Part 7 — Step-by-step test method

When you see the writing prompt in the test, follow these steps:

**Step 1:** Read the prompt carefully. What are you being asked to write about?  
**Step 2:** Think of 4–6 things you want to say. Write them as quick notes.  
**Step 3:** Write your opening sentence.  
**Step 4:** Write your middle sentences, one idea at a time.  
**Step 5:** Write your closing sentence.  
**Step 6:** Check your work using the checklist above.

Write a paragraph (6–10 sentences) on this topic:

> **"Write about why you want to learn English and what you hope to do in the future."**

Use the structure: opening → middle details → closing thought.  
Use at least 3 linking words from Lesson 6.  
Check your work with the checklist above.
