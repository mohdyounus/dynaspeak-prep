# Lesson 14 - Be, Being, Been, Do, Did, Done

**Level:** Elementary  
**Time:** 20-30 minutes  
**Skills:** Grammar, Reading, Speaking

---

## What you will learn

- The difference between **be**, **being**, and **been**
- The difference between **do**, **did**, and **done**
- Where these forms appear in everyday sentences

---

## Part 1 - The verb family of be

| Form | Type | Example |
|------|------|---------|
| be | base form | I want to **be** ready. |
| am / is / are | present forms | They **are** happy. |
| was / were | past forms | We **were** at home. |
| being | -ing form | He is **being** noisy. |
| been | past participle | She has **been** to Rotorua. |

### When do we use **being**?

Use **being** when **be** follows another verb or appears in a continuous form.

- Thank you for **being** patient.
- He is **being** careful today.

### When do we use **been**?

Use **been** after **have / has / had**.

- I have **been** tired all day.
- She has **been** to Dunedin twice.
- They had **been** ready for an hour.

---

## Part 2 - The verb family of do

| Form | Type | Example |
|------|------|---------|
| do | base form / present | I **do** my homework after dinner. |
| does | present with he/she/it | He **does** the shopping. |
| did | past form | We **did** the washing yesterday. |
| done | past participle | She has **done** the test. |
| doing | -ing form | They are **doing** well. |

### Important rule

Use **done** after **have / has / had**.

- I have **done** my work.
- He had **done** the dishes before bed.

Do not use **done** alone as the main past verb.

Incorrect: I **done** my homework.  
Correct: I **did** my homework.  
Also correct: I have **done** my homework.

## Part 3 - Student comparison table

| Word | Use | Example |
|------|-----|---------|
| be | base form | I want to be calm. |
| being | continuous or after preposition | She is being helpful. |
| been | after have/has/had | We have been busy. |
| do | base / present | Do you work here? |
| did | past simple | Did you call me? |
| done | after have/has/had | They have done enough. |

---

## Part 4 - Mini dialogues

### Dialogue 1

A: Why are you so quiet?  
B: I am **being** careful because the baby is sleeping.

### Dialogue 2

A: Have you **done** your homework?  
B: Yes, I **did** it after dinner.

### Dialogue 3

A: Have you **been** to the new library?  
B: Yes, I have **been** there once.

---

## Common mistakes

Incorrect: She has **being** sick all week.  
Correct: She has **been** sick all week.

Incorrect: I have **did** my chores.  
Correct: I have **done** my chores.

Incorrect: He is **been** rude.  
Correct: He is **being** rude.

## Practice

Fill in the gaps with **being**, **been**, **did**, or **done**:

1. She has _____ very busy this week.
2. We _____ our shopping yesterday.
3. Thank you for _____ honest.
4. Have you _____ your writing task yet?
5. He had _____ tired before the game started.

**Answers:** 1. been 2. did 3. being 4. done 5. been
