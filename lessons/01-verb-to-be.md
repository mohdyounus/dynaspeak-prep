# Lesson 1 — The verb "to be"

**Level:** Beginner–Elementary  
**Time:** 15–20 minutes  
**Skills:** Grammar, Speaking, Writing

---

## What you will learn

- How to use **am**, **is**, and **are**
- How to make **positive**, **negative**, and **question** sentences
- How to talk about yourself and other people

---

## Part 1 — Positive sentences

The verb **to be** changes depending on who you are talking about.

| Subject | Verb | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| I | **am** | I **am** a student. |
| You | **are** | You **are** from New Zealand. |
| He | **is** | He **is** a teacher. |
| She | **is** | She **is** happy. |
| It | **is** | It **is** cold today. |
| We | **are** | We **are** friends. |
| They | **are** | They **are** from India. |

### Short forms (contractions)

In speaking and informal writing, we often shorten the verb:

| Full form | Short form |
|-----------|------------|
| I am | I'm |
| You are | You're |
| He is | He's |
| She is | She's |
| It is | It's |
| We are | We're |
| They are | They're |

> 💡 **Example sentences:**  
> *I'm from Pakistan.*  
> *She's a nurse.*  
> *They're at school.*

---

## Part 2 — Negative sentences

To make a negative sentence, add **not** after the verb.

| Subject | Verb + not | Short form | Example |
|---------|-----------|------------|---------|
| I | am not | I'm not | I'm not tired. |
| You | are not | You aren't | You aren't late. |
| He | is not | He isn't | He isn't a doctor. |
| She | is not | She isn't | She isn't from China. |
| It | is not | It isn't | It isn't cold. |
| We | are not | We aren't | We aren't ready. |
| They | are not | They aren't | They aren't students. |

---

## Part 3 — Questions

To ask a question, **move the verb to the front** of the sentence.

| Statement | Question |
|-----------|----------|
| She is a teacher. | **Is** she a teacher? |
| You are from Auckland. | **Are** you from Auckland? |
| They are happy. | **Are** they happy? |
| It is cold. | **Is** it cold? |

### Short answers

| Question | Positive answer | Negative answer |
|----------|----------------|----------------|
| Are you a student? | Yes, I **am**. | No, I'm **not**. |
| Is she from India? | Yes, she **is**. | No, she **isn't**. |
| Are they teachers? | Yes, they **are**. | No, they **aren't**. |

> ⚠️ **Important:** In short answers, we do **not** use contractions in the positive form.  
> ✅ *Yes, I am.* (correct)  
> ❌ *Yes, I'm.* (incorrect)

---

## Part 4 — Practice sentences

Read these sentences out loud. Then write them in your notebook.

**About yourself:**
1. I am [your name].
2. I am from [your country].
3. I am [your age] years old.
4. I am a [your job or student].
5. I am not [something that is not true about you].

**About other people:**
1. My [husband/wife/friend] is from [country].
2. My children are [ages].
3. My teacher is very helpful.

---

## Part 5 — Common mistakes

### Mistake 1: Missing the verb

❌ *She a teacher.*  
✅ *She **is** a teacher.*

### Mistake 2: Wrong form of the verb

❌ *They is students.*  
✅ *They **are** students.*

❌ *I are happy.*  
✅ *I **am** happy.*

### Mistake 3: Double verb

❌ *She is be tired.*  
✅ *She **is** tired.*

## Summary

| Use | Verb | Example |
|-----|------|---------|
| I | am | I am happy. |
| He / She / It | is | She is a teacher. |
| You / We / They | are | They are from Nepal. |
| Negative | isn't / aren't | He isn't tired. |
| Question | Is...? / Are...? | Are you ready? |
