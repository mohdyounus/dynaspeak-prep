# Lesson 13 - Have, Has, Had, and Have Been

**Level:** Elementary  
**Time:** 20-30 minutes  
**Skills:** Grammar, Reading, Writing

---

## What you will learn

- When to use **have**, **has**, and **had**
- The difference between possession and perfect tense
- How forms like **have been**, **has been**, and **had been** work

---

## Part 1 - Have, has, and had for possession

| Subject | Present form | Past form | Example |
|---------|--------------|-----------|---------|
| I / you / we / they | have | had | We **have** two classes today. |
| he / she / it | has | had | She **has** a new job. |

Use **have / has** for now. Use **had** for before now.

Examples:

- I **have** a phone.
- He **has** a car.
- They **had** a meeting yesterday.

---

## Part 2 - Have, has, and had as helping verbs

These words also help make **perfect tenses**.

| Pattern | Use | Example |
|---------|-----|---------|
| have + past participle | present perfect with I/you/we/they | I **have finished** my homework. |
| has + past participle | present perfect with he/she/it | She **has started** work. |
| had + past participle | past perfect | They **had left** before noon. |

---

## Part 3 - Have been, has been, had been

| Form | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| have been | experience or continuing situation until now | I **have been** in New Zealand for two years. |
| has been | same idea with he/she/it | She **has been** busy all morning. |
| had been | situation before another past event | We **had been** friends before we worked together. |

### Read these carefully

| Sentence | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| I **have been** to Wellington. | I visited Wellington sometime before now. |
| She **has been** sick since Monday. | She started being sick before now and still is sick. |
| They **had been** tired before the meeting started. | Their tiredness happened before another past event. |

---

## Part 4 - Quick comparison table

| Form | Job | Example |
|------|-----|---------|
| have | main verb for possession | I **have** a notebook. |
| has | main verb for possession | He **has** a bus card. |
| had | past of have / has | We **had** lunch at 1:00. |
| have been | present perfect of **be** | I **have been** happy lately. |
| has been | present perfect of **be** | It **has been** a long day. |
| had been | past perfect of **be** | She **had been** worried before the exam. |

---

## Common mistakes

Incorrect: She **have** a new bag.  
Correct: She **has** a new bag.

Incorrect: I **has been** busy.  
Correct: I **have been** busy.

Incorrect: They **had been late yesterday**.  
Better: They **were late yesterday**.

Use **had been** only when you compare two past times.

---

## Practice

Choose the correct answer:

1. He _____ a doctor appointment at 3:00. (have / has)
2. We _____ already eaten dinner. (have / has)
3. She _____ very tired since this morning. (has been / had been)
4. They _____ in Christchurch before moving to Auckland. (had been / have been)
5. I _____ a headache yesterday. (had / have)

**Answers:** 1. has 2. have 3. has been 4. had been 5. had
