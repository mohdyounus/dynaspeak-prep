# Lesson 5 — Articles: a, an, the

**Level:** Elementary  
**Time:** 15 minutes  
**Skills:** Grammar, Writing

---

## What you will learn

- When to use **a** and **an** (indefinite articles)
- When to use **the** (definite article)
- When to use **no article at all**

---

## Part 1 — "a" and "an" (indefinite articles)

We use **a** or **an** when we talk about something for the **first time**, or when it is **one of many** (not specific).

| Article | When to use | Examples |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| **a** | before consonant sounds | a book, a car, a teacher, a dog |
| **an** | before vowel sounds (a, e, i, o, u) | an apple, an egg, an idea, an orange, an umbrella |

> 💡 **Important:** It is the **sound**, not the letter, that matters.  
> - *a **u**niversity* (sounds like "you" — consonant sound → use **a**)  
> - *an **h**our* (the h is silent — vowel sound → use **an**)

### Examples in sentences

> *I have **a** daughter and **a** son.*  
> *She is **a** nurse.*  
> *He ate **an** apple for breakfast.*  
> *It was **an** easy test.*

---

## Part 2 — "the" (definite article)

We use **the** when we are talking about something **specific** — something the listener knows about, or something already mentioned.

| When to use "the" | Example |
|-------------------|---------|
| Something already mentioned | *I have a dog. **The** dog is brown.* |
| Something unique (only one) | ***The** sun is very hot today. / **The** sky is blue.* |
| Something specific both people know | *Can you close **the** door? / **The** teacher is kind.* |
| Places that are one of their kind | ***The** Pacific Ocean / **The** North Island* |

### Examples in sentences

> *I went to **the** supermarket this morning.*  
> *My children go to **the** school on our street.*  
> ***The** teacher at DynaSpeak is very helpful.*

---

## Part 3 — No article

We do **not** use an article in these situations:

| When | Example |
|------|---------|
| Plural nouns (general) | ***Books** are important.* (NOT: The books are important) |
| Uncountable nouns (general) | *I drink **coffee** every morning.* |
| Names of countries (most) | *I am from **India**. / She lives in **New Zealand**.* |
| Names of people | ***Maria** is my friend.* |
| Languages | *I speak **English** and **Tagalog**.* |
| Meals | *I have **breakfast** at 7am.* |

---

## Part 4 — Common mistakes

### Mistake 1: Using "a" before a vowel sound

❌ *I ate a apple.*  
✅ *I ate **an** apple.*

### Mistake 2: Missing "the" for specific things

❌ *I went to hospital on left side of road.*  
✅ *I went to **the** hospital on **the** left side of **the** road.*

### Mistake 3: Using "the" with general plurals

❌ *The children need the love.*  
✅ *Children need love.*

## ✏️ Practice exercise

Fill in the gaps with **a**, **an**, **the**, or **–** (no article):

1. I have _____ brother and _____ sister.
2. She is _____ excellent teacher.
3. Can you please open _____ window?
4. I drink _____ tea every morning.
5. He is from _____ Philippines.
6. I saw _____ interesting film last night.
7. _____ sun rises in the east.
8. My children go to _____ school near our home.

**Answers:** 1. a / a 2. an 3. the 4. – 5. the 6. an 7. The 8. the (specific school) or – (general)
