# Lesson 11 - Verb Forms Overview

**Level:** Beginner-Elementary  
**Time:** 20-25 minutes  
**Skills:** Grammar, Reading, Writing

---

## What you will learn

- What **base**, **past**, **past participle**, and **-ing** forms mean
- Why English uses different verb forms in different tenses
- How to recognize common forms like **be / been / being** and **do / did / done**

---

## Part 1 - The four main verb forms

Many English verbs have four common forms.

| Verb family | Base form | Past simple | Past participle | -ing form |
|-------------|-----------|-------------|-----------------|-----------|
| be | be | was / were | been | being |
| do | do | did | done | doing |
| have | have | had | had | having |
| go | go | went | gone | going |
| work | work | worked | worked | working |

### What each form does

| Form | Use | Example |
|------|-----|---------|
| Base form | dictionary form, after modals, after **do/does/did** | I can **go**. Do you **work**? |
| Past simple | finished action in the past | We **went** home early. |
| Past participle | perfect tenses, passive voice | She has **done** her homework. |
| -ing form | continuous tenses, some nouns and adjectives | They are **studying** now. |

---

## Part 2 - Why verb forms change

English changes verb forms to show:

1. **Time**: present, past, future
2. **Aspect**: simple, continuous, perfect
3. **Function**: main verb or helping verb

Compare these sentences:

| Sentence | Why the form changes |
|----------|----------------------|
| I **work** every day. | base form for present simple |
| I **worked** yesterday. | past simple for finished past action |
| I have **worked** here for two years. | past participle after **have** |
| I am **working** now. | -ing form after **am** |

## Part 3 - Verb family examples

### The verb **be**

| Form | Example |
|------|---------|
| be | I want to **be** calm. |
| was / were | They **were** tired. |
| been | She has **been** busy. |
| being | He is **being** polite. |

### The verb **do**

| Form | Example |
|------|---------|
| do | I **do** my homework at night. |
| did | We **did** the test yesterday. |
| done | They have **done** the work. |
| doing | She is **doing** the dishes. |

### The verb **have**

| Form | Example |
|------|---------|
| have | I **have** two children. |
| had | We **had** class last week. |
| had | He has **had** breakfast already. |
| having | They are **having** lunch. |

---

## Part 4 - A quick tense map

| Tense pattern | Example |
|---------------|---------|
| Present simple | She **works** in Auckland. |
| Past simple | She **worked** last Saturday. |
| Future with will | She **will work** tomorrow. |
| Present continuous | She is **working** now. |
| Present perfect | She has **worked** here since 2024. |

---

## Common mistakes

### Mistake 1: Using past simple instead of past participle

Incorrect: I have **went** home.  
Correct: I have **gone** home.

### Mistake 2: Forgetting the helping verb

Incorrect: She **working** now.  
Correct: She **is working** now.

### Mistake 3: Mixing base form and past form

Incorrect: Yesterday we **go** to class.  
Correct: Yesterday we **went** to class.

## Practice

Choose the correct form:

1. They have _____ the lesson. (finish / finished)
2. She is _____ dinner. (cook / cooking)
3. We _____ to the park yesterday. (go / went)
4. I will _____ you tomorrow. (call / called)
5. He has _____ very kind today. (be / been)

**Answers:** 1. finished 2. cooking 3. went 4. call 5. been
