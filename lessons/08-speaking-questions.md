# Lesson 8 — Speaking: common test questions

**Level:** Elementary–Pre-intermediate  
**Time:** 20–25 minutes  
**Skills:** Speaking, Vocabulary

---

## What you will learn

- The most common questions in the DynaSpeak speaking test
- How to give detailed, natural answers
- Tips for speaking confidently

---

## Common topic 1 — Your daily routine

### Teacher might ask:

> *"What do you do every day?"*  
> *"Can you describe your typical day?"*  
> *"What time do you wake up?"*

### Model answer:

> *I wake up at about 6:30 every morning. First, I make breakfast for my husband and children. My children start school at 8:30, so I take them by car. After that, I come home and do the housework — washing, cleaning, and tidying. In the afternoon, I sometimes go to the supermarket or visit a friend. My husband comes home at about 5:30, and we have dinner together at 6 o'clock. In the evenings, I study English and help my children with their homework. I usually go to bed at around 10pm.*

**Key grammar used:** present simple, time adverbs, sequencing words

---

## Common topic 2 — Your family

### Teacher might ask:

> *"Tell me about your family."*  
> *"Do you have children?"*  
> *"Where does your family live?"*

### Model answer:

> *I have a husband and three children. My husband's name is Ahmed and he works as a security guard. My oldest child is 12 years old and goes to intermediate school. My younger two children are 8 and 5. They both go to primary school near our home in Ōtāhuhu. My parents and my brothers and sisters are still in Bangladesh. I miss them very much, but we video call every Sunday.*

---

## Common topic 3 — Your country and culture

### Teacher might ask:

> *"Tell me about your country."*  
> *"What is the food like in your country?"*  
> *"What do you miss about your home country?"*

### Model answer:

> *I come from the Philippines. It is a beautiful country with many islands, warm weather, and friendly people. The food is delicious — we eat a lot of rice, fish, and pork. I really miss my family and the food. Also, life is more relaxed there. But I like New Zealand because it is safe, clean, and there are good opportunities for my children.*

---

## Common topic 4 — Why you want to learn English

### Teacher might ask:

> *"Why do you want to study English?"*  
> *"How will English help you?"*

### Model answer:

> *I want to improve my English for several reasons. First, I want to find a job here in New Zealand and communicate well with my colleagues and customers. Second, I want to help my children with their school work because they sometimes need help reading and writing in English. Third, I want to feel more confident when I go to the doctor, the school, or government offices. I think English will open many doors for me.*

---

## Common topic 5 — Your previous work

### Teacher might ask:

> *"What was your job in your country?"*  
> *"What kind of work are you looking for?"*  
> *"Have you worked in New Zealand?"*

### Model answer:

> *In my country, I worked as a primary school teacher for eight years. I taught children aged 6 to 10. I enjoyed my work very much. When I came to New Zealand, my teaching qualification was not recognised here, so I started looking for other work. At the moment, I am studying English and hoping to find work as a teacher's aide in a primary school. I have also done some volunteer work at my children's school.*

---

## Common topic 6 — Your hobbies

### Teacher might ask:

> *"What do you do in your free time?"*  
> *"Do you have any hobbies?"*  
> *"What do you enjoy doing?"*

### Model answer:

> *In my spare time, I enjoy cooking traditional food from my country. I also like going to the park with my children on weekends. Sometimes I watch films in English to practise listening. I have recently started going to a community group for parents where I meet other mothers and practise speaking English. I enjoy that very much.*

---

## Speaking tips — summary

| Tip | Why it helps |
|-----|-------------|
| Speak in full sentences | Shows grammar knowledge |
| Add detail and examples | Shows vocabulary range |
| Use linking words (first, then, because) | Shows connected thinking |
| Speak at a comfortable speed | Shows confidence, easier to understand |
| Don't stop if you make a mistake | Shows fluency |
| Ask for clarification if needed | Shows communication skills |

## ✏️ Practice exercise

Choose **two** topics from this lesson. Write 5–8 sentences for each one as if you are answering in the speaking test. Then practise saying them out loud.

> 🎤 **Challenge:** Ask a friend or family member to ask you the questions. Try to answer without reading your notes.
