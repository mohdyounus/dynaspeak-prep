# Lesson 6 — Everyday vocabulary

**Level:** Beginner–Intermediate  
**Time:** 20–25 minutes  
**Skills:** Vocabulary, Speaking, Writing

---

## What you will learn

- Key vocabulary about **yourself and your family**
- Words for **daily life**
- Words for **feelings and opinions**
- Useful **linking words**

---

## Part 1 — About yourself

### Personal information

| Word | Meaning | Example sentence |
|------|---------|-----------------|
| nationality | which country you are from | My nationality is Samoan. |
| occupation / job | what you do for work | My occupation is cleaner. |
| residence / address | where you live | My residence is in Māngere. |
| marital status | married, single, divorced | My marital status is married. |
| fluent | speak a language very well | I am not yet fluent in English. |
| background | your history and culture | I have a Filipino background. |
| experience | things you have done | I have experience in childcare. |

### Family words

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| spouse | husband or wife |
| sibling | brother or sister |
| relative | any family member |
| extended family | grandparents, aunts, uncles, cousins |
| household | people living together in one home |
| dependent | a child or person you look after |

## Part 2 — Daily life

### At home

| Word | Example sentence |
|------|-----------------|
| chores | I do chores every morning — washing, cooking, cleaning. |
| tidy up | I tidy up the house after breakfast. |
| grocery shopping | I go grocery shopping twice a week. |
| pick up / drop off | I pick up my children from school at 3pm. |
| budget | We have a weekly budget for food. |
| commute | My husband commutes to work by bus. |

### In the community

| Word | Example sentence |
|------|-----------------|
| neighbourhood | I like my neighbourhood — it is quiet and friendly. |
| community | There is a big Pacific Island community in our area. |
| enrol | I want to enrol in an English class. |
| support | DynaSpeak provides support for new migrants. |
| volunteer | Some people volunteer at the school. |
| resource | The library has many free resources. |

## Part 3 — Describing feelings

| Feeling | Opposite | Example |
|---------|----------|---------|
| happy | sad | I am happy to be in New Zealand. |
| excited | nervous | I am excited about my first class. |
| confident | worried | I am not yet confident speaking English. |

| grateful | ungrateful | I am grateful for the free classes. |
| motivated | unmotivated | I am motivated to improve my English. |

## Part 4 — Opinion and linking words

These words help you connect ideas when speaking and writing.

### To add information

| Word | Example |
|------|---------|
| and | I speak Tagalog **and** some English. |
| also | I **also** want to find a job. |
| as well | I have two children. My husband works **as well**. |
| in addition | **In addition**, I am studying for a certificate. |

### To show contrast

| Word | Example |
|------|---------|
| but | I like Auckland, **but** the weather is cold. |
| however | I enjoy cooking. **However**, I don't have much time. |
| although | **Although** my English is not perfect, I practise every day. |

### To give reasons

| Word | Example |
|------|---------|
| because | I am studying English **because** I want to find a job. |
| so | I studied hard, **so** I feel more confident now. |
| so that | I wake up early **so that** I have time to exercise. |

### To sequence

| Word | Example |
|------|---------|
| first / firstly | **First**, I make breakfast for my children. |
| then | **Then**, I take them to school. |
| after that | **After that**, I do the housework. |
| finally | **Finally**, I have some time to study. |

## Part 5 — Words for the writing test

These phrases are useful when writing a short paragraph about yourself:

| Phrase | Use |
|--------|-----|
| My name is... | introduce yourself |
| I am originally from... | say where you come from |
| I have been living in New Zealand for... | say how long you've been here |
| I came to New Zealand in [year] | give a date |
| I am currently... | say what you are doing now |
| In my spare time, I enjoy... | describe hobbies |
| My goal is to... | say what you want to achieve |
| I am looking forward to... | express positive feelings |

## ✏️ Practice exercise

Choose the best word to complete each sentence:

1. My _______ is India. I came to New Zealand five years ago.  
   a) occupation   b) nationality   c) residence

2. I do all the household _______ every morning before my children wake up.  
   a) chores   b) budgets   c) commutes

3. I feel _______ when I speak English because I make mistakes.  
   a) proud   b) grateful   c) nervous

4. I am studying English _______ I want to get a better job.  
   a) although   b) because   c) however

5. _______, I wake up and make breakfast. _______, I take my children to school.  
   a) However / Also   b) First / Then   c) Because / Finally

**Answers:** 1. b 2. a 3. c 4. b 5. b
