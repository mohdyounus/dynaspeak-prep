# Lesson 9 — Reading strategies

**Level:** Elementary–Pre-intermediate  
**Time:** 25–30 minutes  
**Skills:** Reading, Vocabulary

---

## What you will learn

- Two key reading strategies: **skimming** and **scanning**
- How to answer **comprehension questions**
- How to understand **unknown words** from context

---

## Part 1 — Skimming (reading for the main idea)

**Skimming** means reading quickly to understand the **general topic** of a text. You do not read every word.

### How to skim:
1. Read the **title** (if there is one)
2. Read the **first sentence** of each paragraph
3. Look at any **bold** or **underlined** words
4. Read the **last sentence** of the text

> 💡 **Use skimming to:** understand what a text is about **before** you read the questions.

### Skimming practice

Read the following passage quickly (30 seconds). What is the main topic?

---

*Ahmed is 35 years old and originally from Somalia. He came to New Zealand four years ago as a refugee. He now lives in Māngere with his wife Fadumo and their four children. Ahmed works part-time as a cleaner and is currently studying English at DynaSpeak three mornings a week. His goal is to improve his English so he can find better work and help his children with their homework. Ahmed is also involved in a local football club where he coaches young children on weekends. He says New Zealand has given his family safety and opportunity.*

---

**Main topic:** *(Ahmed's life in New Zealand — his family, work, study, and goals.)*

---

## Part 2 — Scanning (finding specific information)

**Scanning** means moving your eyes quickly over the text to find **specific information** — a name, a number, a date, or a word.

### How to scan:
1. Read the question first — what information do you need?
2. Look for **key words** from the question in the text
3. When you find the key word, slow down and read carefully

> 💡 **Use scanning to:** answer questions that start with Who? What? Where? When? How many? How long?

### Scanning practice

Use the Ahmed passage above. Answer these questions by scanning:

1. How old is Ahmed?
2. Where is he originally from?
3. How many children does he have?
4. What does he do at the football club?
5. How long has he been in New Zealand?

**Answers:** 1. 35 years old 2. Somalia 3. Four children 4. He coaches young children 5. Four years

---

## Part 3 — Answering comprehension questions

After you skim and scan, answer the questions carefully.

### Types of questions

| Question type | What to look for |
|--------------|-----------------|
| Factual (who, what, where, when) | Find the exact information in the text |
| True/False | Check the text carefully — don't guess |
| Vocabulary | Look at the words around the unknown word |
| Main idea | Think about what the whole passage is about |
| Inference | Think about what the writer is suggesting |

### Important rule

> ⚠️ **The answer is usually in the text.** Do not write your opinion or guess. Go back and find the evidence.

---

## Part 4 — Understanding unknown words

When you see a word you don't know:

1. **Look at the words around it** — what type of word is it? (noun, verb, adjective?)
2. **Think about the general topic** — what makes sense?
3. **Look for clues** — examples, lists, or explanations near the word

### Example

> *Ahmed coaches young children at a local football club on weekends.*

You may not know **coaches**. But you can see:
- It comes after a name and before "young children" — so it is a **verb**
- It is at a "football club" — so it is connected to sport
- Ahmed is helping children — so **coaches** probably means *trains* or *teaches*

## Part 5 — Full practice passage with questions

Read the passage carefully, then answer the questions below.

---

**A new life in Auckland**

My name is Leilani. I am 29 years old and I come from Tonga. I moved to Auckland three years ago with my husband Sione and our two daughters. We live in Ōtāhuhu, in South Auckland. My husband works as a builder and I look after our children at home.

I decided to study English at DynaSpeak because I want to find part-time work. I would like to work in a shop or in a café. I also want to help my daughters with their school work because they sometimes come home with assignments I cannot understand.

Life in Auckland is very different from Tonga. In Tonga, everything is slower and more relaxed. Here, life is busy and there is always something to do. But I love the parks and the libraries, and my daughters are very happy at their school. We also go to a Tongan church every Sunday, which helps me feel connected to my culture.

My goal for this year is to complete Level 2 English and then look for a job. I am grateful for the free English classes at DynaSpeak and the support from the teachers.

---

### Questions

1. Where is Leilani from?
2. How long has she been in New Zealand?
3. What does her husband do?
4. Give **two** reasons why Leilani wants to learn English.
5. What does Leilani say is different about life in Auckland compared to Tonga?
6. What is Leilani's goal for this year?
7. What does the word **"assignments"** probably mean in this context?  
   a) food   b) school tasks   c) friends   d) money

---

### Answers

1. Leilani is from Tonga.
2. She has been in New Zealand for three years.
3. Her husband works as a builder.
4. She wants to find part-time work in a shop or café, and she wants to help her daughters with their school work.
5. In Tonga, life is slower and more relaxed. In Auckland, life is busy and there is always something to do.
6. Her goal is to complete Level 2 English and then look for a job.
7. **b) school tasks** — the clue is "daughters come home with" them and she can't understand them, so they are school-related written tasks.
