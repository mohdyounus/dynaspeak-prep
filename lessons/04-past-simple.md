# Lesson 4 — Past simple tense

**Level:** Elementary  
**Time:** 20–25 minutes  
**Skills:** Grammar, Speaking, Writing

---

## What you will learn

- How to talk about **finished actions** in the past
- Regular verbs (add **-ed**) and irregular verbs (must be memorised)
- Negative sentences and questions in the past

---

## When do we use the past simple?

| Use | Example |
|-----|---------|
| A finished action | *I **studied** English yesterday.* |
| A past event | *She **moved** to Auckland in 2021.* |
| A past habit | *He **worked** as a driver for 10 years.* |
| A series of past events | *I **woke up**, **had** breakfast and **went** to work.* |

**Time words** for past simple:
> yesterday / last week / last year / in 2019 / two years ago / when I was young / this morning

---

## Part 1 — Regular verbs (add -ed)

**Structure:** Subject + verb + **-ed**

| Base verb | Past form | Example |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| work | worked | I **worked** in a factory. |
| live | lived | She **lived** in Samoa. |
| study | studied | He **studied** at school. |
| move | moved | We **moved** to New Zealand. |
| arrive | arrived | They **arrived** yesterday. |
| start | started | I **started** a new job. |
| finish | finished | She **finished** work at 5pm. |

### Spelling rules for -ed

| Rule | Examples |
|------|---------|
| Most verbs: add **-ed** | work → worked, play → played |
| Verbs ending in **-e**: add **-d** only | live → lived, move → moved |
| Verbs ending in consonant + **-y**: change y to **-ied** | study → studied, carry → carried |
| Short verbs ending in CVC: double the last consonant | stop → stopped, plan → planned |

---

## Part 2 — Irregular verbs

These verbs do **not** follow the -ed rule. You need to memorise them.

| Base verb | Past form | Example |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| be | was / were | I **was** tired. / They **were** happy. |
| go | went | She **went** to the market. |
| come | came | He **came** to New Zealand in 2020. |
| have | had | We **had** dinner at 7pm. |
| make | made | I **made** a cake. |
| see | saw | I **saw** my friend yesterday. |
| get | got | She **got** a new job. |
| know | knew | I **knew** the answer. |
| say | said | He **said** hello. |
| think | thought | I **thought** it was easy. |
| take | took | She **took** the bus. |
| give | gave | He **gave** me a gift. |
| find | found | I **found** my keys. |
| buy | bought | We **bought** food at the market. |
| eat | ate | They **ate** dinner together. |
| drink | drank | I **drank** coffee this morning. |
| leave | left | She **left** work at 4pm. |
| meet | met | I **met** my neighbour. |
| tell | told | He **told** me the news. |
| feel | felt | I **felt** nervous. |

> 💡 **Tip:** Make flashcards with the base verb on one side and the past form on the other. Practise a few every day.

---

## Part 3 — Negative sentences

**Structure:** Subject + **didn't** (did not) + base verb

| Example |
|---------|
| I **didn't go** to work yesterday. |
| She **didn't study** last night. |
| They **didn't come** to class. |
| He **didn't have** breakfast. |

> ⚠️ **Important:** After **didn't**, always use the **base verb** (not past form).  
> ❌ *She didn't **went** to school.*  
> ✅ *She didn't **go** to school.*

---

## Part 4 — Questions

**Structure:** **Did** + subject + base verb?

| Question | Short answer yes | Short answer no |
|----------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Did** you go to school? | Yes, I **did**. | No, I **didn't**. |
| **Did** she work yesterday? | Yes, she **did**. | No, she **didn't**. |

### Wh- questions

> *Where **did** you live before Auckland?*  
> *What **did** you do yesterday?*  
> *When **did** you arrive in New Zealand?*  
> *Why **did** he leave his job?*

---

## Part 5 — Was / Were (past of "to be")

| Subject | Past of "to be" |
|---------|----------------|
| I | **was** |
| He / She / It | **was** |
| You / We / They | **were** |

| Sentence | Example |
|----------|---------|
| Positive | I **was** very happy. / They **were** tired. |
| Negative | She **wasn't** at home. / We **weren't** ready. |
| Question | **Was** he a doctor? / **Were** you at school? |

---

## Vocabulary for this lesson

| Word | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| ago | before now | I came to NZ two years **ago**. |
| previous | before the current one | My **previous** job was in a factory. |
| used to | a past habit (no longer true) | I **used to** live in India. |
| childhood | when you were a child | In my **childhood**, I lived on a farm. |
| experience | things you have done | I have **experience** in cooking. |

---

## ✏️ Practice exercise

Write the correct past tense form:

1. I _____ (go) to the supermarket yesterday.
2. She _____ (not / come) to class last week.
3. _____ they _____ (live) in Samoa before New Zealand?
4. He _____ (work) as a driver for five years.
5. We _____ (have) a big party last month.
6. I _____ (not / know) the answer.
7. Where _____ you _____ (study) before?
8. My grandmother _____ (make) beautiful food.

**Answers:** 1. went 2. didn't come 3. Did / live 4. worked 5. had 6. didn't know 7. did / study 8. made
