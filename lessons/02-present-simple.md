# Lesson 2 — Present simple tense

**Level:** Elementary  
**Time:** 20–25 minutes  
**Skills:** Grammar, Speaking, Writing

---

## What you will learn

- How to talk about **habits and routines**
- How to use the present simple in **positive**, **negative**, and **question** forms
- The **he/she/it** rule (adding -s or -es)

---

## When do we use the present simple?

We use the present simple for:

| Use | Example |
|-----|---------|
| Daily routines | *I wake up at 7 o'clock every day.* |
| Habits | *She drinks coffee in the morning.* |
| General facts | *Water boils at 100 degrees.* |
| Things that are always true | *The sun rises in the east.* |

**Time words** that go with present simple:
> every day / every week / always / usually / often / sometimes / never / on Mondays / in the morning / at night

---

## Part 1 — Positive sentences

**Structure:** Subject + verb (+ s/es for he/she/it)

| Subject | Verb | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| I | work | I **work** in a shop. |
| You | live | You **live** in Auckland. |
| He | **works** | He **works** every day. |
| She | **lives** | She **lives** in Mangere. |
| It | **starts** | It **starts** at 9am. |
| We | study | We **study** English. |
| They | cook | They **cook** dinner at 6pm. |

### The -s / -es rule

When the subject is **he**, **she**, or **it**, we add **-s** or **-es** to the verb.

| Rule | Examples |
|------|---------|
| Most verbs: add **-s** | work → works, live → lives, eat → eats |
| Verbs ending in -s, -sh, -ch, -x, -o: add **-es** | wash → washes, watch → watches, go → goes |
| Verbs ending in consonant + y: remove y, add **-ies** | study → studies, carry → carries |

> 💡 **Examples:**  
> *She **studies** English every evening.*  
> *He **watches** TV after dinner.*  
> *My mother **goes** to the market on Saturdays.*

---

## Part 2 — Negative sentences

**Structure:** Subject + **do not (don't)** / **does not (doesn't)** + verb

| Subject | Negative helper | Example |
|---------|----------------|---------|
| I / You / We / They | **don't** | I **don't** eat meat. |
| He / She / It | **doesn't** | She **doesn't** like coffee. |

> ⚠️ **Important:** After **doesn't**, the main verb has **no -s**.  
> ❌ *She doesn't works.*  
> ✅ *She doesn't **work**.*

---

## Part 3 — Questions

**Structure:** **Do** / **Does** + subject + verb?

| Helper | Subject | Verb | Example |
|--------|---------|------|---------|
| **Do** | I / you / we / they | work | **Do** you **work** on weekends? |
| **Does** | he / she / it | live | **Does** she **live** in Auckland? |

### Short answers

| Question | Yes | No |
|----------|-----|----|
| Do you speak English? | Yes, I **do**. | No, I **don't**. |
| Does he work here? | Yes, he **does**. | No, he **doesn't**. |

### Question words (Wh- questions)

| Question word | Example |
|--------------|---------|
| Where | Where **do** you live? |
| What | What **does** she do? (= What is her job?) |
| When | When **do** you start work? |
| How often | How often **do** you study? |
| Why | Why **does** he study English? |

## Part 4 — Talking about daily routines

This is one of the most common topics in the speaking and writing test.

### Useful time phrases

| Time | Phrase |
|------|--------|
| Morning | In the morning, At 7am, After breakfast |
| Afternoon | In the afternoon, At lunchtime, After school |
| Evening | In the evening, At 6pm, After dinner |
| Night | At night, Before bed, At 10pm |

### Example: A daily routine

> *I wake up at 6:30 every morning. I make breakfast for my family. My children go to school at 8 o'clock. In the afternoon, I do housework and go shopping. My husband comes home at 5pm. We eat dinner together at 6 o'clock. In the evening, I study English. I go to bed at 10pm.*

---

## Part 5 — Common mistakes

### Mistake 1: Forgetting the -s for he/she/it

❌ *She work in a hospital.*  
✅ *She **works** in a hospital.*

### Mistake 2: Adding -s after doesn't

❌ *He doesn't **works** on Sundays.*  
✅ *He doesn't **work** on Sundays.*

### Mistake 3: Using do instead of does

❌ *Do she live in Auckland?*  
✅ ***Does** she live in Auckland?*

## ✏️ Practice exercise

Fill in the gaps with the correct form of the verb:

1. I _____ (wake up) at 7am every day.
2. She _____ (study) English on Tuesdays.
3. They _____ (not / eat) meat.
4. _____ he _____ (work) on Saturdays?
5. My mother _____ (cook) dinner every evening.
6. We _____ (not / have) a car.
7. What time _____ you _____ (start) work?
8. He _____ (go) to the gym three times a week.

**Answers:** 1. wake up 2. studies 3. don't eat 4. Does / work 5. cooks 6. don't have 7. do / start 8. goes
