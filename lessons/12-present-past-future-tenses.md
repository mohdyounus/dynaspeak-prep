# Lesson 12 - Present, Past, and Future Tenses

**Level:** Beginner-Elementary  
**Time:** 20-25 minutes  
**Skills:** Grammar, Speaking, Reading

---

## What you will learn

- How to tell if a sentence is talking about **now**, **before now**, or **after now**
- Which verb patterns are common in present, past, and future tenses
- How time words help you choose the correct tense

---

## Part 1 - The big idea

| Time | Question | Common tense |
|------|----------|--------------|
| Present | What is true now? What happens usually? | present simple / present continuous |
| Past | What happened before now? | past simple |
| Future | What will happen later? | will + base verb / going to + base verb |

---

## Part 2 - Present tense

Use the present tense for routines, facts, and actions happening now.

| Use | Pattern | Example |
|-----|---------|---------|
| Routine / habit | subject + base verb | I **work** on weekdays. |
| He / she / it habit | subject + verb + s | She **works** at a cafe. |
| Action now | am / is / are + verb-ing | They **are studying** now. |

### Time words for present

**every day, usually, often, always, now, at the moment**

---

## Part 3 - Past tense

Use the past tense for actions that finished before now.

| Use | Pattern | Example |
|-----|---------|---------|
| Finished action | subject + past verb | We **visited** the doctor yesterday. |
| Negative | did not + base verb | He **did not go** to work. |
| Question | did + subject + base verb | **Did** you call your friend? |

### Time words for past

**yesterday, last week, ago, in 2023, this morning**

---

## Part 4 - Future tense

Use future forms for plans, predictions, promises, and decisions.

| Future form | Use | Example |
|-------------|-----|---------|
| will + base verb | prediction, promise, quick decision | I **will call** you tonight. |
| am/is/are going to + base verb | plan or strong intention | We **are going to move** next month. |
| present continuous | fixed arrangement | She **is meeting** the tutor tomorrow. |

### Time words for future

**tomorrow, next week, soon, later, in the future**

---

## Part 5 - Compare the same idea in three times

| Time | Sentence |
|------|----------|
| Present | I **study** English at DynaSpeak. |
| Past | I **studied** English last year. |
| Future | I **will study** again next term. |

| Time | Sentence |
|------|----------|
| Present | She **is cooking** now. |
| Past | She **cooked** last night. |
| Future | She **is going to cook** tonight. |

---

## Common mistakes

Incorrect: Tomorrow I **went** to class.  
Correct: Tomorrow I **will go** to class.

Incorrect: Yesterday she **doesn't work**.  
Correct: Yesterday she **didn't work**.

Incorrect: He **will goes** home later.  
Correct: He **will go** home later.

---

## Student table - tense signal words

| Signal word | Likely tense | Example |
|-------------|--------------|---------|
| every day | present simple | I walk every day. |
| now | present continuous | I am walking now. |
| yesterday | past simple | I walked yesterday. |
| last night | past simple | We watched TV last night. |
| tomorrow | future | We will study tomorrow. |
| next year | future | They are going to travel next year. |

## Practice

Fill in the correct form:

1. Right now, I _____ dinner. (cook)
2. We _____ to Hamilton last weekend. (drive)
3. She _____ her mother every Sunday. (call)
4. They _____ a new flat next month. (move)
5. _____ you work yesterday? (do)

**Answers:** 1. am cooking 2. drove 3. calls 4. will move / are going to move 5. Did
