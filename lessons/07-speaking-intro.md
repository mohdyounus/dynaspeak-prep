# Lesson 7 — Speaking: how to introduce yourself

**Level:** Beginner–Elementary  
**Time:** 20 minutes  
**Skills:** Speaking, Vocabulary

---

## What you will learn

- How to introduce yourself confidently
- What information to include
- How to speak in full sentences (not just single words)

---

## Why speaking matters in the placement test

The speaking section is a short, friendly conversation with a DynaSpeak teacher. It usually takes 5–10 minutes. The teacher wants to hear you speak so they can understand your level.

> 💡 **Remember:** The teacher is not trying to catch you out. They want to find the best class for you. Speak naturally and honestly.

---

## Part 1 — What to include in your introduction

A good self-introduction covers these five areas:

| # | Topic | Example |
|---|-------|---------|
| 1 | Name | My name is Priya. |
| 2 | Country of origin | I am from Sri Lanka. |
| 3 | How long in New Zealand | I came to New Zealand two years ago. |
| 4 | Family / home | I live in Papatoetoe with my husband and three children. |
| 5 | What you do / are studying | I am currently looking for work. / I am studying at DynaSpeak. |

---

## Part 2 — Model introductions

### Beginner level

> *My name is Ali. I am from Pakistan. I live in Māngere. I have a wife and two children. I speak Urdu. I want to learn English.*

### Elementary level

> *My name is Ali Hassan. I am from Lahore, in Pakistan. I moved to Auckland one year ago with my family. I live in Māngere with my wife and two sons. In Pakistan, I worked as a tailor. Here in New Zealand, I am looking for work. I am studying English because I want to communicate better.*

### Pre-intermediate level

> *My name is Ali Hassan. I originally come from Lahore, Pakistan, which is a large city in the east of the country. I have been living in Auckland for about a year now. I came here on a family visa with my wife and two sons who are currently attending primary school. Before I came to New Zealand, I worked as a tailor for fifteen years. I am hoping to find work in the clothing or retail industry here. I am studying English so that I can improve my communication and find better opportunities.*

> 📝 **Notice** how each version gets longer and uses more complex grammar — but all three are clear and honest.

---

## Part 3 — Sentence starters for introductions

| Situation | Sentence starter |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Your name | *My name is... / I'm...* |
| Your country | *I am from... / I originally come from... / I was born in...* |
| How long in NZ | *I came to New Zealand... ago. / I have been living here for...* |
| Where you live | *I live in... / I am currently living in...* |
| Your family | *I live with my... / I have... children.* |
| Your work | *I work as... / I used to work as... / I am looking for work as...* |
| Why you want English | *I am studying English because... / so that...* |
| Your goal | *I hope to... / My goal is to...* |

---

## Part 4 — Common speaking mistakes and how to fix them

### Mistake 1: One-word answers

❌ Teacher: *"Where are you from?"*  
❌ Student: *"Pakistan."*

✅ Better: *"I am from Pakistan. I am from the city of Lahore."*

---

### Mistake 2: Stopping when you make a mistake

❌ Stopping and saying nothing.

✅ Better: *"Sorry, let me say that again... I am... I have been here for two years."*

---

### Mistake 3: Translating directly from your language

❌ *"I have two years in New Zealand."* (direct translation)  
✅ *"I have been in New Zealand for two years."*

---

## Part 5 — Useful phrases for the speaking test

### If you don't understand

> *"Sorry, could you please repeat that?"*  
> *"I'm sorry, I didn't understand. Could you say it again slowly?"*  
> *"Could you please explain what you mean?"*

### If you need time to think

> *"Let me think for a moment..."*  
> *"That's a good question. I think..."*  
> *"Hmm, I'm not sure, but I think..."*

### To give an example

> *"For example..."*  
> *"Like... / Such as..."*  
> *"What I mean is..."*

---

## Part 6 — Practice out loud

Read the following questions. Practise answering each one out loud. Try to speak for at least 3–5 sentences for each answer.

1. Can you tell me a little about yourself?
2. Where are you from originally?
3. How long have you been in New Zealand?
4. What did you do in your country?
5. Why do you want to learn English?
6. What do you do in your free time?
7. Tell me about your family.

> 🎤 **Tip:** Record yourself on your phone. Listen back and notice if you speak clearly, if your sentences are complete, and if you used correct grammar.

---

## ✏️ Writing exercise

Write a short introduction about yourself (5–8 sentences). Use the sentence starters from Part 3. Include your name, country, how long in NZ, family, and why you want to learn English.
