## Passage 1 — "Starting over"

Read the following passage and answer questions 1–8.

---

*My name is Nour. I am 41 years old and I come from Egypt. I came to New Zealand five years ago with my husband Karim and our three children — two sons and a daughter. We live in Henderson, in West Auckland.*

*Before I came to New Zealand, I worked as a secondary school teacher in Egypt for twelve years. I taught mathematics and science. When I arrived here, I found it very difficult to have my teaching qualification recognised. I tried for two years but was not successful.*

*I now work part-time as a teacher's aide at a primary school near our home. I enjoy working with the children very much, but the pay is low and the hours are limited. My goal is to gain full registration as a teacher in New Zealand one day.*

*I started studying English at DynaSpeak two years ago. My English was already at an intermediate level, but I wanted to improve my professional communication — writing reports, speaking with parents, and attending school meetings with confidence. The teachers at DynaSpeak have been very helpful and the classes are free, which is wonderful for our family budget.*

---

## Passage 2 — "Letter to a friend"

Read the following letter and answer questions 9–15.

---

*Dear Fatima,*

*I hope this letter finds you well! I am writing to tell you about my life in Auckland.*

*I have been here for eight months now. The city is very beautiful — there are many parks, beaches, and hills. The people are friendly and welcoming. However, I have found it quite difficult to adjust. The weather is very different from home — it is often cold and rainy, even in summer! I miss the warm sunshine and the markets in our town.*

*I have started English classes at a school called DynaSpeak. The classes are free and the teachers are patient and kind. I go three mornings a week. I was placed in Level 2, which is for elementary learners. I feel I am improving slowly but steadily.*

*I have also joined a community group for women from our country. We meet every fortnight on a Friday evening. It is lovely to speak our language and share food together. It helps me feel less lonely.*

*I have not found work yet. My husband has a job in construction, so we can manage, but I would like to find something part-time — perhaps in a café or a shop. My English is still not strong enough for an office job.*

*I hope to visit you next year if we can save enough money for the ticket. Please give my love to your family.*

*With warm wishes,*  
*Amara*

---

- a) Two years  
- b) Twelve months  
- c) Eight months  
- d) Three months

- a) The food and the shopping centres
