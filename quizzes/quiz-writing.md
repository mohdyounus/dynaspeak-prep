## Section D — Written tasks

> ✏️ **Instructions:** Write your answers in your notebook. There is no single correct answer — focus on using clear sentences, correct grammar, capital letters, and full stops.

---

**Task 1 — Short paragraph (6–8 sentences)**

Write about yourself. Include:
- Your name and where you are from
- How long you have been in New Zealand
- Your family
- What you do (work, study, or home duties)
- Why you want to learn English

**Checklist before you finish:**
- [ ] Capital letter at the start of every sentence
- [ ] Full stop at the end of every sentence
- [ ] Names and places are capitalised
- [ ] I used at least one linking word (because, and, but, also)
- [ ] I answered everything in the prompt

---

**Task 2 — Describe your daily routine (5–7 sentences)**

Write about what you do every day. Use the present simple tense. Use at least two time words from this list: *in the morning, then, after that, in the afternoon, in the evening, finally*.

**Example opening sentence:** *Every morning, I wake up at [time] and...*

## Writing task — what teachers look for

When your teacher reads your written paragraph, they will notice:

| Area | What they look for |
|------|-------------------|
| Content | Did you answer the question? |
| Grammar | Are your verb tenses correct? |
| Vocabulary | Do you use a range of words? |
| Punctuation | Capital letters and full stops? |
| Coherence | Can they understand your writing easily? |
